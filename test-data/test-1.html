Championship Structure
The championship consists of these divisions (the highest is on the top), each division has some number of leagues. Each league consists of 7 players.

Grandmaster - 1 league
Master - 2 leagues
Platinum- 4 leagues
Gold - 8 leagues
Silver - 16 leagues
Bronze - 32 leagues
Wood - up to 64 leagues

Each player will play four 4-player games and three 3-player games (7 total games), over approximately 2 months. Every player in a league will meet everyone else exactly 3 times (twice in 4-player games, once in 3-player games). 

Scoring
Each position scores the following amount of points: 
3-player game: 5/2/0
4-player game: 6/3/1/0

If players are tied (have the same amount of culture), they split the points evenly.
Every player who runs out of time will be placed last with score 0 points independently of the score of AI who replaced them (example for 3-player game: when AI won, it will receive 0 points and the better human player will receive 5 points, the worse human player 2 points).
This will not be reflected directly on this web, final standings will be done in Google Docs manually (after end of championship).

League Standings
League standings are calculated by comparing the following, in order:

  1. Points
  2. In case of tie between exactly two players, better is that player who finished more times on higher place in head to head games
  3. Average percentage of winner's score from all games
  4. Position in previous season

These tiebreakers are not supported directly on this web, final standings will be done in Google Docs manually (after end of championship).

Promotion and Relegation
Winner of the league will be promoted one division up.
Last two players in the league will be relegated one division down.

Sportsmanship and Best Play

  • Every person can have only 1 account in the championship. 
  • Hidden information should remain secret. Do not use it to influence your opponents' actions (don't ask another player to seed an event because you know that Rats is on the top of event deck, don't disclose someone's military cards after looking at them with Ian Fleming, etc.).
  • Be friendly and nice to your opponents. Insulting a player in chat will result in player disqualification from all the tournaments organized by pajada (International Championship, Intermezzo Championship and Mixed Duel) without any additional warning.
  • Don't seek advice when you play tournament games. You can ask your friend generic questions like do you like the Library of Alexandria or Pyramids. But sharing screenshots, asking for exact actions to be taken is prohibited.
  • Do not purposefully lose to help someone else or for any other reason (e.g. no attacking others/resigning to help a friend, no declaring wars and then disbanding all military units). 
  • If you feel you will finish last, and don't want to try anymore, resign (do not abandon the game). 
  • Every player who runs out of time will be placed in the last place and score 0 points independently of the score of AI who replaced him/her.
  • Players who run out of time for 3 times in the same season will be placed last in their league and relegated. Players will also not be allowed to play next season. 
  • Having more accounts in the championship or purposefully losing will disqualify a player from the championship for next season and that player will have to start from the lowest division.
  • If you come up to situation which is unsportsmanlike you can report it to pajada@gmail.com. Player's Committee consisting of JCleek, IirionClaus, Palino, LaoHuang and pajada will decide the final rulling.

pajada: nuvolaz Yes, usually all 2nd places make it to Bronze, but it is not guaranteed. It depends on how many players stop playing. (21 days ago)
Lothar: If a player times out, and the AI controlled player finishes ahead of another player, do actives players move ahead of the timed out player in finishing place for that game? (20 days ago)
pajada: Lothar Yes, they all move in front of the timed out player. All timed out players also always receive 0 points. (20 days ago)
KarlThomas: A couple of questions regarding resignations. If several players resign in the same game, does the order of the resignation decide the final standings? Specifically, will the first to resign be placed last, while the second to resign is placed on third place in a four player game? Additionally, if one player times out, he is placed last. If someone at a later stage resigns, will they then be placed on third place in a 4p game? (14 days ago)
pajada: KarlThomas Order of resignations is preserved in final scoring - so first resigned player will be 4th, second will be 3rd, etc. Players who run out of time are always placed last and score 0 points. Example: Let's say player A and player B run out of time, player C resigned and player D finishes on 3rd place (behind two AIs). Scoring will be this: player D - 6 points player C - 3 points player A - 0 points player B - 0 points (14 days ago)
